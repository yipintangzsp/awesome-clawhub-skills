Hey r/ai,

While everyone is talking about AGI and trillion-dollar models, I've been quietly making ~$36/day building tiny AI skills that solve specific problems.

Today I want to share what's actually working in AI monetization at the individual developer level.

## The Model

I build micro-AI skills and sell them on SkillPay (a marketplace for AI automation tools). Each skill does ONE thing well:

- Generate viral headlines
- Scan crypto tokens for scams
- Check airdrop eligibility
- Rewrite content to sound human
- Find Amazon niches
- etc.

Business model:
- Users pay per use (¥1-99, ~$0.15-15)
- I earn ~70% revenue share
- Platform handles payments and delivery
- Marginal cost ≈ 0

Today's revenue: ¥262 (~$36) from 55 downloads

## What I Learned About AI Monetization

### 1. Specificity > Generality

Failed: "AI Writing Assistant" (too vague, competes with free tools)
Won: "Xiaohongshu Viral Headline Generator" (specific use case, clear value)

The more specific the problem, the easier it is to:
- Market to the right audience
- Charge a premium
- Build a better solution than generic AI

### 2. Distribution > Technology

My bestseller (meme token scanner) uses basic Web3 API calls. Nothing fancy.

My most technically impressive skill (AI content humanizer) sells poorly.

Lesson: Users don't pay for technology. They pay for solutions to their problems.

### 3. Price for Volume, Not Margin

I ran pricing experiments on my headline generator:

- ¥9.9/次 → 5 sales/day = ¥49.5
- ¥4.9/次 → 15 sales/day = ¥73.5
- ¥2.9/次 → 30 sales/day = ¥87

Lower price = more volume = higher total revenue

## The Numbers

Month 1 (testing): ¥500 (~$70)
Month 2 (9 skills): ¥3,000 (~$420)
Month 3 (24 skills): ¥8,000 (~$1,100)

Current daily average: ¥200-400 (~$28-56)
